# Workflow Failure Points

## Failure Point 1 — Inactive Coverage

### Problem
Coverage is inactive on the date of service, but the patient is still seen.

### Downstream Impact
- Claim denial
- Delayed reimbursement
- Patient billing confusion
- Manual rework for billing staff

### Prevention Strategy
Verify active coverage through the payer portal before the appointment is finalized.

---

## Failure Point 2 — Incorrect Member ID

### Problem
The member ID entered during registration does not match payer records.

### Downstream Impact
- Claim rejection
- Eligibility mismatch
- Delayed claim processing

### Prevention Strategy
Validate member ID and subscriber information during intake.

---

## Failure Point 3 — Wrong Payer Selected

### Problem
The incorrect payer or plan type is selected during registration.

### Downstream Impact
- Claim routed incorrectly
- Payment delay
- Additional billing correction work

### Prevention Strategy
Review payer name, plan type, and billing requirements before service.

---

## Failure Point 4 — Coordination of Benefits Not Reviewed

### Problem
Primary and secondary insurance order is not confirmed.

### Downstream Impact
- COB denial
- Delayed payment
- Multiple claim resubmissions

### Prevention Strategy
Confirm coordination of benefits before the patient encounter.

---

## Failure Point 5 — Authorization Requirement Missed

### Problem
The payer requires authorization, but the requirement is not identified before service.

### Downstream Impact
- Authorization denial
- Delayed reimbursement
- Increased A/R workload

### Prevention Strategy
Review payer authorization rules before appointment confirmation.

---

## Failure Point 6 — Patient Responsibility Not Communicated

### Problem
The patient is not informed about deductible, copay, or coinsurance responsibility before the visit.

### Downstream Impact
- Patient dissatisfaction
- Collection difficulty
- Billing confusion

### Prevention Strategy
Provide financial responsibility estimates before service.

---

## Core Operational Insight

Eligibility verification is not just an administrative task. It is a revenue protection workflow that affects reimbursement timing, denial prevention, patient trust, and operational efficiency across the entire revenue cycle.
